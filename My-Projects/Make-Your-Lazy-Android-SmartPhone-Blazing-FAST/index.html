Tips To Make Your Lazy Android SmartPhone Blazing FAST &apm; Optimize Battery
 by Abhisek Mazumdar • December 20, 2016

There are plenty of App out there which promise to make your Phone Faster. Do they really work ?

Apps like Clean Master, 360 security, du speed booster, cm booster are all time trending Apps in Google play store. We all blindly trust those apps and use it daily. But very sadly, you're just wasting your time in them and also Your Phones battery life. Every Battery Saver App or RAM Saver App in the play store boasts themselves to save battery but the reality is itself drinks about 50–100 MB of RAM on your smart phone continuously which affects your phone and gradually it destroys your phone life.

In the Other Hand

There are battery saver's that needs Root Access and they come in handy like Greenify. It's a great deal and this helps to free up your RAM as well as redeem around 20–40% of your battery life. Android Marshmallow and above version have an amazing features known as Doze Mode which saves you a lot and lot of battery at sleep. Well, not all Android user are very much familiar root access.

Don't you feel helpless ?

Don't worry, I'm here to make things simpler not complicated. Just follow my few simple tips and make your lazy phone blazing fast. After very long experience with android and running few experiments on different android phones, I have complied the most efficient ways to make Your Android to run like Usain Bolt.

• Keep a Clean home screen

i. If you choose to use live wallpaper then consider replacing it with a good static image.
ii. Remove unused icons and limit your Widgets, I will suggest don't use any home screen widgets.
The less cluttered your home screen is, the better in terms of performance.

• Try some clean third-party Launcher

Heavy-handed in-build launchers like those of Samasung Smart Phones added many unwanted animation and high graphics design. This type of Launcher in-build as well as few third-party, consumes the RAM and Battery. My suggesting is to use a simple Launcher like:
i. Nova Launcher
ii. Google Now Launcher
iii. Arrow Launcher
iv. Cheetah Launcher
There are still many good launcher, just keep playing with different launcher according to your need.

• Turn off or reduce animations

Sounds Complicated ? But it's not. Android is heavily customizable, although not all the option are opened to user. You need to Geekifyed yourself. So let's get Started  by lowering down the Animations rate. To do so you need to enable Developer Mode in your Android SmartPhone.

★Please Note, don't play with the other option in the Developer Menu, you can end up messing up your phone Settings.★

Go to Settings > About phone and scroll down to the System section to look for Build number. Tap on it seven times and you should see a message about being a developer(Video tutorial). Now, you can now go back to the previous menu and you should see Developer options listed under System. Head in there and scroll down to find Window animation scale, Transition animation scale, and Animator duration scale. Tap each in turn and set it to .5x(The Best Option) or off. Pick an option and find out what suits you, if you don’t like just go back in and change the values again.

• Uninstall or disable unused apps

We play out with so many Apps, we keep installing them and then forget it after using it for few days. Every app ones installed occupies some space in your phone storage and also in the RAM. And if RAM is being use that implies battery draining. I will suggest you to Uninstall all the unused and unwanted apps. Some times you can come across few apps which can't be uninstalled (Pre installed bloatware). Simply just Disable those apps.

You should also take a look in Settings > Apps at the Running tab. Some apps want to be running all the time and they can seriously impact on performance. Think carefully about whether you need what’s listed there.

• Restart and Reset regularly

Re-Start the phone regularly, will clean up the unwanted temporary files created by various apps. But then also few Temp files in the catch partition can't be removed by just a restart. You need to do a periodic Factory Reset, to clearing all your smartphone partition cache, System cache, and other junk files which are deposited in your smartphone memory.

Don't worry about your personal data. As Always I'm here to make things simpler not complicated. Every Data in your External SD Card will be safe. But contact, installed app, SMS will be needed to backup before doing Factory Reset. You can use Super Backup & Restore, just don't format the External SD card. Using of cloud storage is more preferable for permanent & automatic backup, using Google Photos, Google Contacts, Google Calendar. Just setup these apps in your phone and they will automatically keep uploading your Photo, Video, Contacts. • Make sure you’re up to date

Many brand like Samsung, Xiaomi, Asus, Lenovo, Micromax provide OTA(Over The Air) i.e. bug fixes and general improvements update for the Android. This OS update are important and it will be better if you download and apply this update.

The same logic applies to apps, so fire up the Play Store, open the menu at the top left and tap My apps and make sure all the apps you use are up to date.
